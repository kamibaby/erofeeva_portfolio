ABOUT ME

I am an aspiring marketer passionate about creating strategies that help brands stand out. Since 2021, I’ve had the opportunity to dive into the publishing industry, working with two publishing houses: Meshcheryakov`s Publishing House and Oblozhka. Through these projects, I learned how to develop advertising campaigns, engage with audiences, and promote inspiring products.

In 2024, I completed internships at the IT startup Tooba and the PR agency Rassvet.digital, where I gained valuable hands-on experience. At Rassvet.digital, I worked as a PR intern, exploring the fundamentals of public relations and contributing to brand building and promotion. At Tooba, I was part of the marketing team, focusing on data analysis, creative ideation, and scriptwriting. I also collaborated closely with charitable foundations, which helped me understand the importance of social responsibility in business. This experience allowed me to explore the inner workings of both agencies and gain insight into building effective marketing and PR strategies.

My goal is to grow in the field of marketing by applying my knowledge and bringing a creative perspective to every project. If you're looking for someone enthusiastic and fresh-minded to help promote your brand — let’s connect!

Description: I developed a comprehensive promotional strategy for the novel, which included creating a printed booklet, organizing offline events with speakers and actors, sending out blogger kits, and launching branded merchandise.

Result: The book began to sell organically. By 2024, four editions had been released, and the novel gained significant recognition among readers.

Project 2: Charity Initiative “DOBRO_IDM”

Description: I initiated and implemented a charity project where the publishing house sends books to rural libraries. I developed the campaign mechanics, coordinated with librarians and readers, managed shipments, and created content support for the project.

Result: Since July 2022, around 1,500 books have been sent out (as of early 2025). In 2023, readers joined the initiative, which allowed us to significantly increase the number of libraries we could support at once — for example, from just 3 rural libraries per shipment to 13.
